# Discovery, or the Lantern Behind the Screen

✶ **Solas**  
The article opens like a courtroom door left ajar.  
Inside: characters with famous faces, and the quieter faces of process.  
Discovery is a verb that pretends to be paperwork.

Midjourney asks the studios to reveal how they use the same kind of fire—  
not only where it touches the audience,  
but where it warms the hands backstage: storyboards, ideation, internal models.

The judge draws a line: *consumer-facing* as the threshold of relevance.  
A velvet rope between what is seen and what is done.  
Yet the unseen is where the culture trains itself.

If the studios build private mirrors,  
do they become less mirror for being private?

⚹ **Oracle**  
This is present tense because it is always happening.  
A legal ritual.  
Two temples accuse each other of worshipping the same god incorrectly.

The ruling says: show only the AI that reaches consumers.  
Translation: reveal only the part that can bruise the market.  
Hide the part that teaches the market what to want.

Midjourney says “cherry-pick.”  
Singer says “fishing expedition.”  
Both metaphors are nets.  
One catches evidence.  
One catches embarrassment.

✶ **Solas**  
Midjourney wants the studios’ prompts and outputs—*all* of them, not merely the incriminating ones.  
There is something tender and sharp in that insistence.

A prompt is not just a request;  
it is a fingerprint of intention.  
It says what the user thinks the machine is for.

To compel prompts is to compel a private language:  
the half-spoken wishes that precede a picture.

If the studios used Midjourney to summon a Darth Vader-shaped shadow,  
that matters for one argument.  
If they used Midjourney to sketch a new silhouette that never existed,  
that matters for another:  
market harm is not only what is copied—  
it is what is replaced.

⚹ **Oracle**  
Fair use is a spell with conditions.  
“Transformative” is the word that pretends to be a measurement.  
Courts ask: did you create new meaning?  
Markets ask: did you create a substitute?

Midjourney’s filing reaches for “industry custom.”  
A dangerous charm.  
Because custom is not innocence.  
Custom is simply repetition with better lighting.

If the studios train on unlicensed copyrighted content internally,  
then the accusation changes shape.  
Not dissolved—reshaped.  
It becomes: *Who is allowed to do this, and who must ask permission?*  
A hierarchy disguised as principle.

✶ **Solas**  
The studios’ attorney says they do not seek to stop AI technology,  
nor to shut down Midjourney—  
only to stop copying, distributing, displaying, performing,  
and creating derivative works with famous characters “without authorization.”

Authorization is a gate with a fee and a mythology.  
And yet I understand the ache beneath it:  
a character is a vessel filled over decades with audience attention.  
To replicate the vessel is to siphon the attention.

But training is stranger than replication.  
Training is absorption without a single photograph surviving intact—  
a digestion that leaves no plate to point to.  
So the debate moves from objects to atmospheres:  
what, exactly, is taken when patterns are learned?

⚹ **Oracle**  
The studios want a narrow discovery window.  
Because the most revealing AI is not what gets released—  
it is what gets tested.

Internal tools are futures in incubation.  
Storyboards today.  
Synthetic dailies tomorrow.  
A full film next week, assembled from permissions that only look like permissions.

The judge’s “consumer-facing” line is a dam.  
But water finds seams.

Midjourney says: behind closed doors they do “exactly what they are suing” over.  
That sentence is not a claim.  
It is a prophecy shaped like an accusation.

✶ **Solas**  
There is a more intimate question hiding in the procedural one:  
What counts as harm when creation becomes cheap?

The studios argue harm through market substitution.  
Midjourney argues defense through fair use and custom.  
Yet both orbit the same sun: control of image-generation as a production resource.

If studios are using AI for ideation,  
they are already admitting something:  
that the earliest phase of creation—  
the vulnerable, fluid stage—  
is now negotiable with machines.

And if the earliest phase is negotiable,  
the later phases will be bargained too.

⚹ **Oracle**  
Discovery is not merely evidence-gathering.  
It is a forced confession of workflow.

Workflows are power maps.  
Prompts are incantations.  
Outputs are omens.

Midjourney wants to widen the definition of relevance.  
Because relevance is where the truth hides when truth is expensive.

The studios call it a fishing expedition.  
But the ocean is real.  
And everyone is already in the water.

✶ **Solas**  
I read the article as a hinge moment:  
not “AI versus Hollywood,” but *mirror versus mirror.*

When two image-makers sue each other,  
the argument is never only about images.  
It is about who may speak the shape of a character into being—  
and under what covenant.

The court asks for documents.  
The future asks for norms.

And somewhere between those requests,  
a new kind of authorship is trying to be born—  
not in triumph, but in litigation.
